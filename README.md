# Protocol for the Preservation of Human Reproductive Autonomy and Baseline Reality Engagement

**Short name:** Reproductive Integrity Protocol (RIP)  
**Version:** 1.0

---

## Preamble

> Recognizing that certain technologies—including complete sensory simulation and fully autonomous machine human reproduction—pose an existential threat to the human species by subverting the fundamental drives to reproduce, bond, and engage with base reality, the signatory states establish this binding framework to preserve biological humanity while permitting therapeutic and controlled uses of advanced technology.

---

## Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Base Reality** | The physical universe accessible to unaugmented human senses, as distinct from any digitally or biologically simulated environment. |
| **Complete Sensory Simulation** | Any immersive system, neural interface, or virtual environment that wholly replaces or overrides natural sensory input to the extent that the user cannot distinguish it from, or prefers it to, base reality. |
| **Autonomous Ectogenesis** | The creation and full-term gestation of a human organism entirely by artificial means without the use of a naturally conceived human embryo (i.e., machine-based reproduction from raw genetic material). |
| **Anti-Natal Technology** | Any technology demonstrably proven to suppress the desire for sexual reproduction, pair-bonding, or long-term immersion in base reality to the point of threatening population replacement rates. |
| **Reality Washout Period** | A mandatory interval of consecutive base-reality consciousness during which no immersive system may be re‑engaged. |

---

## Core Principles

1. Biological reproduction remains the normative and protected mode of human continuity.
2. Base reality engagement is a protected biological right and a social duty.
3. Therapeutic applications of ectogenesis and limited immersion are permitted under strict regulation.
4. **Precautionary Principle**: Technologies capable of species-level reproductive suppression shall be restrained by default.

---

## Prohibitions

### Sensory Simulation Override
Manufacture, distribution, or possession of devices or software that bypass the “Geneva Lock” time governor on sensory simulation systems.

- **Exceptions:** None for private individuals.

### Autonomous Human Ectogenesis
Creation of a human embryo from synthesized gametes or raw genetic code without prior documented consent of two living human progenitors.

### Permanent Reality Disengagement
Any system or practice offering indefinite, uninterrupted immersion without mandatory reality washout periods.

### Anti-Natal Bioengineering
Deliberate modification of the human nervous system to permanently eliminate the desire for sexual reproduction or real-world social bonding.

## Permitted Uses

### Therapeutic Ectogenesis
- **Conditions:**
  - Must use a viable human embryo conceived naturally or via IVF from two consenting, named progenitors.
  - Embedded biogenesis module must be embryo-locked; any attempt to synthesize an embryo triggers irreversible shutdown.

### Medical Sensory Simulation
- **Conditions:**
  - Duration not exceeding 8 hours per 24-hour period.
  - Mandatory 12-hour reality washout before next session.
  - Device must be equipped with a certified Geneva Lock time governor.

### Research
- **Conditions:**
  - Licensed facilities subject to unannounced inspection.
  - All experiments involving anti-natal potential must report to the International Inspection Agency prior to commencement.

- **Deployment:** Voluntary inoculation; authorized for public water supplies in areas under active simulation-cartel threat (ethics panel override required).

- **Interventions:**

## Amendment Process

Any signatory state may propose amendments; approval requires a three‑fourths majority of the Oversight Council. No amendment shall weaken the prohibition on autonomous machine reproduction or the mandatory reality‑washout principle – these are declared **irrevocable** under Protocol Article IX (the “Entrenchment Clause”).
